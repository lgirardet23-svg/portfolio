Road Sign Structural Analysis

Finite Element Analysis (FEA) and optimization of a cantilevered motorway sign post.

Category: CAD, Mechanical Analysis (Erasmus Project)

1. Road Sign Drawing.

2. Creo model showing applied loads (wind, weight) and anchorage.

3. Creo results showing maximum Von Mises Stress (left) and Deflection (right).

4. Simulation comparing square vs. circular cross-sections (same area).

5. Simulation comparing hollow vs. solid circular cross-sections (same area).

6. Close-up of stress and table of results at the pole base (Points A-D).

7. Summary table comparing Creo, Ansys, and Mathematical stress results.

Project Goal 🎯

The objective was to design and analyze a lightweight cantilevered steel post structure capable of supporting two motorway road signs. The design had to minimize weight while enduring complex loads, including compressive, torsional, bending, and shear stresses, primarily caused by the signs' weight and a maximum wind speed of 100 km/h (473 N/m²).

Key Stages & Technologies Employed 🛠️

  • **FEA Modeling:** The structure was modeled in CAD (Creo) and analyzed using both **Creo Mechanica** and **Ansys FEA**. The final material selected was Structural Steel due to its strength-to-weight ratio and resistance to deflection.
  • **Optimization Study:** Simulations were conducted to optimize the cross-sectional shape for minimal stress and weight. The circular cross-section showed lower Von Mises stress (10.66 MPa) compared to the square cross-section (11.87 MPa) for the same area.
  • **Hollow vs. Solid Design:** Further optimization showed that a hollow circular post (Outer D=200mm, Inner D=180mm) significantly reduced stress and achieved a total weight of 346 kg.
  • **Mathematical Validation:** Stress values were calculated mathematically at four highly stressed points at the base of the pole. The maximum structure Von Mises stress from the FEA was 73.16 MPa, resulting in a Safety Factor of 2.9 against the structural steel's yield strength of 250 MPa.
  • **Analysis:** The maximum stress was located on the underside of the top 90° angle, which is the predicted point of failure.

**Context:** Mechanical Design Lab, Erasmus Semester at **Technological University Dublin**, Ireland (Jan 2024).

Nanomaterials for a Sustainable Future

Research on the applications of nanomaterials for environmental remediation.

Category: Research, Environmental Engineering (Erasmus Project)

1. **Project Poster:** Overview of pollution and nanoremediation.

Project Goal 🎯

The objective of this group research project was to explore the pivotal role of nanomaterials in achieving global sustainable development goals. The project covered four primary areas where nanotechnology is driving innovation: Sustainable Manufacturing, Energy (Storage/Production), Pollination, and Pollution (Air/Water/Soil).

My Contribution: Pollution and Environmental Remediation 🌊

My work focused entirely on the Nanomaterials and Pollution section, analyzing how industrialization and agricultural activities increase contamination, and how nanomaterials offer more effective and less toxic clean-up methods than conventional approaches.

Water Decontamination Focus: Nanophotocatalysts

I concentrated specifically on water decontamination using Nanophotocatalysts (like TiO₂, Fe₂O₃, ZnO). These materials are effective because their unique properties (surface, chemical, magnetic) make them highly reactive.

  • **Mechanism:** When exposed to light, the nanophotocatalyst is excited, generating electron-hole pairs that lead to the formation of powerful oxidizing agents (like hydroxyl radicals). These radicals break down complex organic pollutants (pesticides, pharmaceuticals) into harmless compounds.
  • **Solution Recovery:** I highlighted the importance of Magnetic Nanoparticle Composites (MNPC), which allow for the easy separation and recovery of the photocatalysts from the treated water using an external magnet, addressing a key challenge in cost-effectiveness and delivery.

Air Pollution and Remediation

The analysis also covered air pollution, including the use of **nanofilitration systems** (with pores smaller than 1-10 nanometers) to filter fine particulate matter and the use of **nanocatalysts** in tailpipes to make chemical reactions more efficient and less polluting.

**Context:** Mechanical Engineering Research Project, Erasmus Semester at **Technological University Dublin**, Ireland (Jan 2024).

Design and Feasibility Study of an Opto-Mechatronic Intra-Oral Examination Device (ISIFC Project).

Category: CAD / Biomedical Engineering / Mechatronics

Project Goal 🎯

The core objective is to design a modern opto-mechatronic device for controlling the quality of brushing and inter-dental cleaning in children and adults. This device must offer the user information on the real condition of every visible surface of each tooth and inter-dental space.   • **F6: Bulk: The system's footprint/size must be as small as possible.
  • **F7: Environment: The system must be transportable and function in a bathroom setting.
  • **F8: Data Management: Acquisition must be as fast as possible, and the display must allow for post-processing for optimal management, archiving, and interpretation of information.
  • **F9: Eco-Design: Particular attention must be paid to eco-design (sharing, collective, family use).

State-of-the-Art Report on innovative medical devices for people with blindness or near blindness.

Goal of the Project 🎯

The main goal of this project was to conduct a comprehensive state-of-the-art review of **innovative Medical Devices (MD)** aimed at people with blindness or near blindness. The ambition was to determine how these cutting-edge technologies can promote inclusion and autonomy in the daily lives of patients.

Abstract

After exposing the anatomical aspect of the visual system and associated pathologies, this report presents a complete state-of-the-art of **innovative medical devices (MD) for people with blindness or near blindness**, with an objective of inclusion and autonomy. It distinguishes two main families: **non-implantable devices** (connected glasses, smart canes, or GPS assistants), and **implantable devices** (visual neuroprostheses). The document analyzes their functioning, clinical maturity, and performance. Particular attention is paid to technological innovations (AI, image processing, brain-machine interfaces) and regulatory issues (CE marking, EU regulation 2017/745). While non-implantable devices are readily available and improve daily life, implantable devices, though complex and mostly experimental, are followed with special attention as they promise to "restore a visual sensation". The study concludes by suggesting that the integration of new technologies should respect ethical, social, and environmental values.

**Group Project:** Realized by Odile Slifi, Lucas Girardet, and Lucas Dordor (ISIFC 1A).

Syringe & Packaging System

Full modeling from single parts to final assembly, including design validation.

Category: CAD / Biomedical Engineering (Group Project)

1. Detailed view of the syringe (CAD modeling).

2. Closed packaging box, containing the syringes.

3. Open box showing the packaging.

4. Modeling of the needle with its safety cap.

Project Goal 🎯

This project was completed to meet the specific needs of veterinarians during herd vaccination campaigns, focusing on safety and practical weekly conditioning of medical supplies. The goal was to design a complete system: a safety needle and its associated thermal packaging.

Key Stages & Technologies Employed 🛠️

  • **Needle Design (Monobloc & Multi-part):** The initial design was based on the standard **20-Gauge** (0.91mm outer diameter, 4cm length) used for bovine subcutaneous injections. The modeling progressed from a "monobloc" concept to the final assembly of two distinct parts: the biocompatible metal cannula and the plastic insert (hub).
  • **Safety Cap Mechanism:** A rotating safety cap was designed with minimal support zones (`crans`) that engage the plastic insert only when turned to a specific alignment. This system allows the cap to be removed without detaching the needle for injection (Phase 2), and subsequently reattached and rotated to safely lock and remove the used needle for disposal (Phase 3).
  • **Packaging Modeling:** A simple, secure box was modeled in Polystyrene to ensure thermal insulation during transport, a requirement essential for vaccine integrity. The box was dimensioned to hold seven complete, non-assembled syringe-needle sets.
  • **Validation:** The final packaging was validated through assembly checks and a cross-section view to confirm zero interference between the seven sets when the box is closed.

**Group Project:** Realized by Lucas Girardet and Schoen Elise (ISIFC 1A).

Project Goal 🎯

The main objective was to design and model a functional object or structure using the **Tensegrity principle** (a structural system using continuous tension and discontinuous compression). The design had to meet contest constraints requiring **Aluminum** as the primary material. By aligning with a leisure/camping theme, the Ping-Pong table was selected as the best compromise.

Key Stages & Technologies Employed 🛠️

  • **Research and Inspiration:** The project started by adapting an existing Tensegrity artwork, which served as the foundation for transforming the structure into a functional table.
  • **Design and Sketching:** The concept was first translated into sketches, adapting the initial structure and defining dimensions (e.g., total height of 75 cm) to meet standard table tennis specifications.
  • **CAD Modeling (3D):** The entire design was modeled in 3D, respecting the structural integrity of the tensegrity system (cables replacing rigid connections).
  • **Details and Finalization:** Accessories such as the net and suspension cables were modeled for the final assembly. The base was specifically designed to include integrated storage slots for balls and rackets.

**Technical Challenge:** Successfully translating the tension/compression structure into a stable and usable table design, while adhering to dimension and material constraints.

Paddleboard Pressure-Regulating Valve

Design and prototyping of an essential safety system to prevent overinflation in inflatable paddleboards.

Category: CAD / Design (Group Project)

1. Final CAD model of the complete valve system (closed view).

2. Internal CAD view showing the piston mechanism and spring housing.

3. 3D-printed prototype of the valve body, ready for assembly.

4. Physical prototype of the internal pressure regulation mechanism.

5. Project summary poster, including methodology and results.

Project Goal 🎯

This project involved the complete design, prototyping, and validation of a **passive pressure-regulating valve** for inflatable stand-up paddleboards. The primary goal was to create a robust and reliable system capable of automatically releasing excess air pressure. This prevents damage to the board, which can occur when internal pressure rises due to sun exposure and heat, while the user is out on the water.

Key Stages & Technologies Employed 🛠️

The core challenge was to design a valve that maintains a precise maximum pressure (e.g., 15 PSI) using a mechanical spring system, while remaining watertight and easy to integrate.

  • Conceptual Design: Analysis of pressure/temperature effects on inflatable structures and spring mechanics calculation to define the release threshold.
  • CAD Modeling (Catia V5): Detailed modeling of the valve housing and all moving parts (piston, gaskets, spring seat), focusing on high-precision assembly.
  • Prototyping: Used 3D printing (FDM) for the housing and small parts. Machining was considered for the final production of high-stress components.
  • Validation & Iteration: Bench testing of the prototype revealed initial leakage issues around the joint plane, leading to a crucial re-design phase focusing on gasket integration and surface finish to ensure reliable pressure retention.

**Group Project:** Realized by Lucas Girardet and Ambéis Matéo (L2 Mécanique 2022/2023).

Project Goal 🎯

These are examples of other projects conducted during my Bachelor's studies in Engineering. The goal was to develop practical proficiency in Computer-Aided Design (CAD), 3D modeling, and techniques like rapid prototyping (laser cutting) and machining. The main objective was to integrate manufacturing constraints (such as play, tight fit, and tolerancing) directly into the design phase.

Project 1: Goodie Design & Manufacturing (PMMA)

The goal was to design and manufacture a two-part promotional item (goodie) in PMMA (polymethyl methacrylate) using laser cutting.

  • CAD: Modeling of the plate and the base in Catia, including the use of Sketch Tracer to integrate a personalized logo.
  • Key Challenge: Ensuring a tight, secure fit of the plate into the base.
  • Solution: The actual thickness of the PMMA plate was measured (2.8mm instead of 3mm nominal). The slot in the base was precisely adjusted from 3mm to 2.7mm in the laser machine's software to compensate for material thickness variation and achieve the desired tight fit.

Project 2: Layered Sphere Prototyping (Plywood)

This project focused on recreating a 40mm diameter sphere from 3mm thick plywood plates by stacking laser-cut circular layers.

  • Strategy: Determining the number of layers (15 final layers) and calculating the diameter of each successive disc to closely approximate a spherical shape.
  • Key Challenge: Ensuring a tight, secure fit between the discs and the central assembly axis.
  • Solution: The central axis was modeled in Catia. The diameter of the inner holes in the discs was adjusted to 2.55mm (for a theoretical 2.5mm axis). This precise adjustment ensured the required tight fit for a stable stacking of the layers.

Software Project: Ludo Game (Jeu des Petits Chevaux)

Full implementation of the classic French board game in Java.

Category: Programming / Software Development

1. Main Menu and Selection of Game Modes.

2. Solo Mode Setup: Difficulty Selection.

3. Two-Player Race Mode Setup.

4. In-Game View (Console Mode).

Project Goal 🎯

The core objective was the comprehensive implementation of the classic French board game, **Jeu des Petits Chevaux** (similar to Ludo), in Java as part of an Algorithmic and Programming coursework. The application needed to support variable difficulty levels and two distinct game modes: a **Solo mode** with a limited number of moves, and a **Two-player Race mode**.

Key Stages & Technologies Employed 🛠️

  • **Multi-Version Development:** The application features both a text-based non-graphic (console) version and a **graphical user interface (GUI) version**.
  • **Difficulty Scaling:** Three difficulty levels, Beginner, Pro, and Legend, were implemented. The chosen level dynamically adjusts the size of the game board and sets the maximum number of moves allowed in Solo mode.
  • **Special Case Logic:** The game includes **Bonus and Penalty** squares that enable players to advance, rewind, receive an extra roll, or lose their turn, respectively. Additionally, players must land exactly on the final square, otherwise, they return to their previous position.
  • **Persistence & History:** The console version saves a detailed score history of all games played (Solo and Duo) into separate files, including player names, final board matrices, and the number of turns taken.

**Technology Focus:** Java, Console I/O, Basic GUI (EcranGraphique library), Randomization (`Math.random()`), File Management (I/O).
