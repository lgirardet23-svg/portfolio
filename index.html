Bachelor's Projects Showcase

A collection of CAD, Machining, and Prototyping projects from my engineering undergraduate degree.

CAD | Design | Prototyping

Goodie Project (2D & 3D)

Goodie Assembly

Laser Cut Sphere Design

Sphere Prototype

Final Sphere

This selection includes two main projects realized during my engineering Bachelor: the **"Goodie" Project** (CAD/Machining) and the **"Sphere" Project** (Prototyping/Laser Cutting).

The **Goodie Project** focused on designing a two-part promotional object in PMMA using CATIA V5, with a key challenge being to achieve a tight fit (montage serré) between the upper plate and the base through precise laser cutting adjustments.

The **Sphere Project** involved using laser cutting to create a 40mm sphere by stacking multiple plywood discs. The core challenge was calculating the number of layers and their respective diameters to approximate the sphere's curvature, while also ensuring the discs were tightly mounted on a central axis.

These projects strengthened my skills in **CAD modeling (CATIA V5)**, **technical drawing**, **prototyping**, and understanding **manufacturing constraints**.

Innovative MDs for Visual Impairment

Comprehensive state-of-the-art report on non-implantable and neuroprosthetic medical devices for the blind.

Biomedical | Research | State-of-the-Art Report

Cover Page (Excerpt)

Sensory Substitution Device

Neuroprosthesis Example

This comprehensive state-of-the-art report (in English) was conducted during my studies at ISIFC and focused on the technological landscape of medical devices designed to aid individuals suffering from severe visual impairment or blindness.

The report detailed various innovative solutions, ranging from **non-implantable sensory substitution devices** (which translate visual data into auditory or tactile feedback) to advanced **neuroprosthetics** (such as retinal, optic nerve, and visual cortex implants).

The work involved rigorous documentation, synthesis of clinical data, analysis of regulatory requirements (MDR 2017/745), and a review of the underlying neuroscience, particularly the concept of **cerebral plasticity**. This project significantly enhanced my research skills and understanding of the biomedical device development pipeline.

Paddleboard Safety Valve

Passive valve system to automatically prevent overinflation of stand-up paddleboards due to solar heating.

CAD | Design | Prototyping | Mechanical

CAD Model (PTC Creo)

Detailed View

Prototyping Phase

Final Prototype

This creativity and prototyping project aimed to solve a real-world problem: the overinflation and potential explosion of stand-up paddleboards left in the sun, due to internal pressure increases from rising temperature.

I designed a **passive safety valve system** using **PTC Creo** that automatically releases excess air when the internal pressure exceeds a safe threshold, then seals again. The valve can also be used for initial inflation.

The work included **functional analysis**, **CAD modeling**, **technical drawing**, and **3D printing** the prototype. The main challenge was ensuring the system was fully airtight and reliably released pressure at the specified value.

Syringe & Packaging System

Full CAD modeling of a safety needle and a custom 7-unit thermal packaging box for veterinarians.

CAD | Biomedical | Packaging

Safety Syringe CAD

Packaging Box CAD

This project involved advanced CAD modeling in **SolidWorks**. The objective was to design a complete system comprising both a medical device and its specialized packaging.

The medical device component was a **safety syringe/needle** designed to prevent accidental needle-stick injuries.

The packaging component was a custom **7-unit thermal packaging box** for veterinarians, designed to maintain a specific temperature and securely hold the syringes for transportation and storage. This project enhanced my ability to handle complex, multi-part CAD assemblies and apply design principles to both medical devices and their logistical elements.
