CAD Project: Syringe & Packaging System

Full modeling from single parts to final assembly, including design validation.

Category: CAD / Biomedical Engineering

Syringe and capped multi-block needle assembly.

Custom 7-unit Polystyrene packaging box (for thermal insulation).

Final condition check with 7 sets inside, validating absence of interferences (cross-section view).

This project was developed to meet the specific needs of veterinarians during herd vaccination campaigns, focusing on safety and practical weekly conditioning.

The initial step involved the **CAD modeling of the needle and its safety cap**, first as a single "monoblock" part, then broken down into its industrial components: the biocompatible metal cannula and the plastic insert. This decomposition was essential for accurately representing the manufacturing constraints.

The second phase focused on **packaging design**. I modeled a simple, secure box in Polystyrene to hold seven complete, unassembled syringe-needle sets. The choice of Polystyrene addresses the manufacturer's requirement for thermal insulation during transport.

**Validation** was carried out through assembly checks (syringe + capped needle) and a cross-section view of the final packaging containing all seven sets, ensuring **zero interference** between the parts during transport.

Paddleboard Pressure-Regulating Valve

Details on the pressure-regulating valve project.

Category: CAD / Design

Detailed view of the internal pressure regulation mechanism.

As part of my Bachelor's engineering project, I co-led the design, prototyping, and validation of a pressure-regulating valve system for inflatable stand-up paddleboards. The objective was to automatically prevent overinflation caused by solar heating during use. The project involved a full design cycle from needs analysis to final assembly using CAD modeling, 3D printing, and machining techniques.
