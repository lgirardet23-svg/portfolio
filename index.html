CAD Project: Syringe & Packaging System

Full modeling from single parts to final assembly, including design validation.

Category: CAD / Biomedical Engineering

1. Detailed view of the syringe (CAD modeling).

2. Closed packaging box, containing the syringes.

3. View of the open box with the assembled syringe and needle sets.

4. Modeling of the needle with its safety cap.

This project was completed in collaboration with a colleague to meet the specific needs of veterinarians during herd vaccination campaigns, focusing on safety and practical weekly conditioning.

My initial step involved the CAD modeling of the needle and its safety cap, first as a single "monoblock" part, then broken down into its industrial components: the biocompatible metal cannula and the plastic insert. This decomposition was essential for accurately representing the manufacturing constraints.

The second phase focused on packaging design. I modeled a simple, secure box in Polystyrene to hold seven complete, unassembled syringe-needle sets. The choice of Polystyrene addresses the manufacturer's requirement for thermal insulation during transport.

Validation was carried out through assembly checks (syringe + capped needle) and a cross-section view of the final packaging containing all seven sets, ensuring zero interference between the parts during transport.

Paddleboard Pressure-Regulating Valve

Details on the pressure-regulating valve project.

Category: CAD / Design

Detailed view of the internal pressure regulation mechanism.

As part of my Bachelor's engineering project, I co-led the design, prototyping, and validation of a pressure-regulating valve system for inflatable stand-up paddleboards. Software Project: Ludo Game (Jeu des Petits Chevaux)

Full implementation of the classic French board game in Java.

Category: Programming / Software Development

1. Overview of the board game layout (Graphical version).

2. Difficulty selection menu (Beginner, Pro, Legend).

3. Non-graphic console mode during a two-player game.

This academic project involved the comprehensive implementation of the classic French board game, Jeu des Petits Chevaux (similar to Ludo), in Java. The project was developed by Lucas Girardet, in collaboration with a colleague, as part of the Algorithmic and Programming coursework.

The application features both a non-graphic (console) version and a graphical user interface (GUI) version. The game supports two primary modes: a Solo mode (where the player must reach the finish line in a minimum number of turns) and a Two-player Race mode (the first player to reach the final square wins).

Players can select between three difficulty levels—Beginner, Pro, and Legend—which dynamically adjust the size of the game board and the maximum number of allowed moves in Solo mode. The game also includes special squares that act as Bonus or Penalty zones, allowing players to jump ahead or be sent back.

A core feature is the file management system used to save a complete history of all games played. This includes player names, the final board matrix, and the winner, stored in separate files for solo and duo games.
